We are forty-two booksellers in two cities, selecting the books we'd want on our own shelves — for readers who still believe ideas need time.

Our story

From one shelf of books to eighteen thousand.

Inkwell & Spine began on a corner street in 2013, when two booksellers and a designer pooled their savings to open a single-room shop with 500 carefully chosen titles. Twelve years later the store has grown into a premium book retailer — but the corner-shop ethic hasn't moved.

We still order short runs, still pay our authors properly, still close every Sunday with a long lunch and a new reading list.

What we believe

Six principles, kept in the open.

01

Slow over fast

Better to sell one book a reader will keep than ten they'll forget.

02

Craft is the brief

Every edition we stock should reward the hand as well as the eye.

03

Pay the author

Fair margins to publishers are the foundation of fair publishing.

04

Read, then stock

The first read is for us. The second is for the customer.

05

The object matters

Paper, weight, binding, ink, the smell — the book is the gift.

06

Quiet wins

The best books make themselves small enough to be picked up.

The reading room

A library, not a stockroom.

Our shop's central space is a 4,000-volume reading room — every book read by someone on the team, every spine a small argument. Customers are welcome to visit, take a chair by the window, and stay as long as the light holds.

The press partners

Printed by friends,
shipped from a small floor.

We work with a 70-year-old family press in Antwerp on our own-brand editions. They use vegetable inks and uncoated paper sourced within 200km. We've never moved to mass production. We never will.
